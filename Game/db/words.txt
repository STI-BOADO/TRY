earth-The third planet from the sun and the only known planet with living organisms.
moon-The only natural satellite of Earth and the fifth largest moon in the solar system.
mars-The fourth planet from the sun, also known as the Red Planet due to its reddish appearance.
venus-The second planet from the sun, also known as the Morning Star or the Evening Star.
jupiter-The fifth planet from the sun and the largest planet in the solar system.
saturn-The sixth planet from the sun with its distinctive rings made of ice and rock particles.
neptune-The eighth planet from the sun and the farthest known planet from Earth until 2006.
uranus-The seventh planet from the sun with its unusual tilt that causes it to rotate on its side.
pluto-A dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, once considered the ninth planet until its reclassification in 2006.
mercury-The closest planet to the sun and the smallest planet in the solar system.
galaxy-A large group of stars, gas, and dust held together by gravity, such as the Milky Way.
comet-A small icy body that releases gas and dust as it approaches the sun, forming a visible coma and tail.
asteroid-A small rocky object in space, often found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
star-A celestial object that emits energy in the form of light and heat, such as the Sun.
telescope-An instrument used to observe and magnify distant objects in space.
solarSystem-The collection of planets and other objects that orbit around the Sun.
constellation-A group of stars forming a recognizable pattern in the sky, such as Orion or the Big Dipper.
blackhole-A region of space with a gravitational pull so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape it.
nebula-A cloud of gas and dust in space, often where new stars are forming.
supernova-An explosive event that occurs when a star exhausts its fuel and undergoes a catastrophic collapse.
astronomy-The scientific study of celestial objects and phenomena.
andromeda-The closest galaxy to the Milky Way, located approximately 2.5 million light years away.
aurora-A natural light display in the sky caused by charged particles from the sun colliding with Earth's atmosphere.
celestial-Relating to the sky or outer space.
cluster-A group of stars or galaxies located close to each other in space.
cosmic-Relating to the universe or cosmos.
crater-A bowl shaped depression on a planet or moon, often caused by impact from an asteroid or meteorite.
eclipse-A phenomenon where one celestial body passes between another and the observer, such as a solar or lunar eclipse.
exoplanet-A planet that orbits a star outside of our solar system.
ganymede-The largest moon in the solar system, located orbiting Jupiter.
gravity-The force of attraction between objects in space, such as planets and moons.
hubble-The Hubble Space Telescope, a large telescope used to observe and image distant objects in space.
internationalSpaceStation-A man made object in space used as a research laboratory and observatory.
kuiperBelt-A region of the solar system beyond Neptune that contains many small icy objects, such as Pluto and Eris.
meteorite-A rock fragment from outer space that has survived its passage through the Earth's atmosphere and hit the Earth's surface.
orbit-The path that an object takes as it revolves around another object in space, such as a planet around a star.
planetarium-A building or room equipped with a projector to display images of the stars and planets on a dome-shaped screen.
rocket-A vehicle that moves in space by expelling gas out of its back, such as a spacecraft or missile.
satellite-An object in space, either natural or man made, that orbits around a larger object, such as a planet or moon.
universe-The totality of all matter, energy, and space, including galaxies and the contents of intergalactic space.
cosmology-The study of the structure and evolution of the universe.
darkMatter-Matter that does not emit, absorb, or reflect light, but can be detected through its gravitational effects.
exoplanetaryScience-The study of planets outside our solar system.
asteroidBelt-A region of the solar system between Mars and Jupiter that contains many small rocky objects.
interstellar-Relating to the space between stars.
lunarEclipse-An eclipse that occurs when Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon.
meteorShower-A celestial event where a large number of meteors appear to originate from the same point in the sky.
neutronStar-The collapsed core of a massive star that has gone supernova, composed almost entirely of neutrons.
observatory-A building or facility equipped with telescopes and other instruments for observing celestial objects.
pulsar-A highly magnetized, rotating neutron star that emits beams of electromagnetic radiation.
quasar-A luminous active galactic nucleus powered by accretion onto a supermassive black hole.
redGiant-A star in the late stages of its life that has exhausted the hydrogen fuel in its core and expanded in size.
solarFlare-A sudden, intense burst of energy from the Sun's surface that can cause disruption on Earth.
telescopeArray-A collection of telescopes that work together to provide higher resolution and sensitivity than a single telescope.
ultraviolet-Relating to electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength shorter than that of visible light but longer than X-rays.
variableStar-A star that changes in brightness over time due to internal processes or external influences.
whiteDwarf-A small, extremely dense star that has exhausted most of its nuclear fuel and is no longer undergoing fusion reactions.
xray-A form of high energy electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength shorter than that of ultraviolet radiation.
yellowDwarf-A main sequence star, similar to the Sun, that emits a yellowish white light.
zenith-The point in the sky directly overhead an observer.
